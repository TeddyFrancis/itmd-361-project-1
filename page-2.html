Teddy Francis Playing Career

MIHA Hockey

Teddy Francis currently plays in the Midwest Inline Hockey Association (MIHA). He wears number 6 and plays forward for the tier 2 Windy City Syndicate Black (formerly South). The upcoming 2017-2018 season will be his second with the team.

During his rookie campaign, Francis amassed a total of 21 points in 21 games. 10 of those points were goals and 11 were assists. Number 6 was a very integral part of the teams penalty killing efforts which was much needed because of the teams inability to stay out of the penalty box. South led the league in penalty minutes by a large margin.

The tier 2 South team lost in the semifinals during championship weekend to their rivals to the North, Windy City Syndicate North. The final score of the game was 7-5. This was only the second time these teams matched up during the season. The other was a 4-3 win for the South during the championship game at the first tournament in Chicago.

Following the season, Nick Anderson of the Homer Glen Hockey Magazine caught up with Francis. He was able to get some time to ask questions during locker clean out day. Anderson: "You were not able to make the championship game at the final weekend this year. Being the ultimate goa of your team, does that make this season a failure?" Francis: "Not at all. We played well enough to give ourselves a chance to win and the puck did not bounce our way that game. To say this year was a failure is foolish. We won 1 tournament and made the championship game in 2 others. We lost to the team who won the championship last year. No one is hanging their heads in this locker room." Anderson: "You managed to average one point per game in your rookie campaign. Can you comment on the success that you were able to have?" Francis: "I was fortunate to be on such a good team that had some very experienced players on the roster. Those guys made it very comfortable for me to fit into the team and go out on the ice and play my game." Anderson: "I appreciate the time, just one more question. What do you guys have to do to achieve your ultimate goal next season?" Francis: "As a team, we just need to keep putting in the work at the practice rink and in the weight room. We are close, but there is still a lot of work to be done to get to where we ultimately want to be."

Playing History

Francis spent his youth hockey career playing at Arctic Ice Arena in Orland Park, IL. He started playing roller hockey, but quickly switched to ice hockey at a young age. At the Arctic Ice Arena, he developed the skills that would help him in his future playing locations.

He then spent his high school career playing for the Providence Catholic Celtics. Francis played in varsity games all four years, playing both junior varsity and varsity his freshman and sophomore years before being full-time varsity his upper class years. As a senior, he was a team captain and was also awarded the Frank Cavallone Award given to the senior hockey player with the highest GPA.
